# Data and AI Manager

## About the Position
An excellent opportunity is available for a Data and AI Manager to join the Information Technology Department at Australia's pre-eminent biomedical research institute.

The Data and AI Manager is a newly established role responsible for designing, implementing, and scaling the organisation's enterprise data and artificial intelligence capabilities. The role leads development of a fit-for-purpose data environment, shapes data governance practices, and identifies opportunities to embed responsible AI into business and research operations.

This position is both strategic and hands-on, working across the organisation to uplift data maturity, develop foundational infrastructure and processes, and ensure safe and ethical use of data and AI technologies. The role also provides strategic advice to the Executive and Senior Leaders on data governance and management best practices.

## Key Responsibilities
- Develop and implement an enterprise data strategy aligned with organisational priorities and regulatory requirements.
- Establish data governance policies and frameworks, including data ownership, data quality standards, and lifecycle management.
- Design and implement a scalable data environment to support business intelligence, analytics, agentic and other forms of AI.
- Identify and lead implementation of AI and machine learning initiatives that solve business problems or enhance decision-making.
- Develop and manage project plans for data and AI initiatives, including change management and training.
- Establish and promote responsible AI principles aligned with industry best practice and ethical standards.
- Ensure compliance with relevant data and AI-related laws and frameworks (for example Australian Privacy Act, ISO standards, and AI governance principles).

## Selection Criteria
### Personal Qualities
- High integrity and confidentiality.
- Strong collaboration across teams and committees to achieve shared goals.
- Strong attention to detail and effective time management, including prioritisation of critical work.
- Exceptional oral, written, and interpersonal communication with technical and non-technical stakeholders.
- Proven planning and delivery for complex programs, services, and projects.
- Proven ability to lead change and innovation while fostering continuous improvement.

### Knowledge and Skills
- Tertiary qualifications in Information Systems, Engineering, Computer Science, Data Science, or related field.
- Demonstrated experience leading data or AI initiatives in complex organisations.
- Experience embedding data governance, data architecture, and data platform technologies.
- Practical knowledge of AI/ML tools, techniques, and frameworks, including model use and integration into business workflows.
- Experience in research, medical, tertiary education, or government sectors is highly regarded.
- Familiarity with ethical AI frameworks (for example OECD, EU AI Act, Australian AI Ethics Principles).
- Exposure to data privacy, security, and compliance requirements (for example ISO 27001, NIST, GDPR).
- Experience working with executive and senior stakeholders to uplift organisational maturity in data governance and management.
