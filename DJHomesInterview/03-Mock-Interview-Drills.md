# 03 - Mock Interview Drills

## Drill 1: 20-minute panel simulation
- 2 minutes: Opening pitch.
- 12 minutes: 6 core KSC questions (2 minutes each).
- 4 minutes: Deep follow-up on one technical decision.
- 2 minutes: Your closing statement.

## Drill 2: Technical depth pressure test
Practice concise responses to:
- Why medallion over direct star schema only?
- How would you design MDM for customer and property domains first?
- How do you prevent Power BI self-service chaos?
- How do you monitor pipeline quality in real time and batch?
- What is your integration pattern decision tree for API vs ETL vs event?

## Drill 3: Executive communication test
Answer each in 45 seconds:
- What business outcomes can we expect in 90 days?
- What are the top delivery risks and mitigations?
- Why should we trust your roadmap?

## High-scoring answer checklist
Use this checklist before each practice:
- I stated a concrete scenario with scale and complexity.
- I clearly explained my personal ownership.
- I gave architecture and engineering detail, not tool listing.
- I included measurable outcomes.
- I linked the example to DJ Homes context.

## Red-flag checklist
If any item is true, revise the answer:
- Too generic; no domain detail.
- Too much "we" and not enough "I".
- No metrics, no outcomes, or no transferability.
- No governance/quality/control discussion.

## 7-day rehearsal plan
Day 1:
- Finalize facts and metrics in all model answers.
- Practice opening pitch 10 times.

Day 2:
- Rehearse Q1-Q4 with timer (2 minutes each).
- Record and refine clarity.

Day 3:
- Rehearse Q5-Q8 with timer.
- Improve business outcome wording.

Day 4:
- Run full 8-question mock interview.
- Fix weak transitions and jargon.

Day 5:
- Run technical depth pressure test.
- Prepare one whiteboard-style architecture explanation.

Day 6:
- Run executive communication test.
- Practice concise risk/mitigation answers.

Day 7:
- Final full simulation in interview clothes and setup.
- Light revision only; no new content.

## Interview-day execution
- Use concise, structured answers (90-120 seconds each).
- Pause before answering to frame the scenario.
- Keep focus on business outcomes plus technical rigor.
- End with confidence in first 90-day delivery.
