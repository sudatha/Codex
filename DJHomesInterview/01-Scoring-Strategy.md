# 01 - Scoring Strategy

## What this role needs
Role: Senior Manager, Data & Integration (first data hire, reporting to CIO, hands-on plus strategic).

Panel focus areas from PD and KSC:
- Build an enterprise data capability from scratch.
- Deliver practical MDM, governance, and quality improvements.
- Architect and implement warehouse/lakehouse with medallion layers.
- Lift Power BI quality, performance, semantic modeling, and self-service.
- Integrate multiple operational systems through repeatable patterns.
- Communicate clearly with executives and delivery teams.

## 100% score formula
Use this structure in every answer:
1. Context: Organization scale and problem severity.
2. Your leadership: What you personally owned and decided.
3. Technical depth: Architecture, tools, patterns, and controls.
4. Business impact: Measurable outcomes.
5. Transferability: Why this approach fits DJ Homes.

## Evidence bank from your CV (use repeatedly)
- 15+ years in data, BI, and platform leadership.
- Architecture ownership across 250+ endpoints (Abano).
- Fabric medallion implementation and mirroring (Attekus).
- Power BI uplift: DAX optimization, semantic models, deployment improvements.
- 8 TB SQL Server to Azure migration with controlled cutover (Whanau Tahi).
- Strong SQL, ADF/SSIS-aligned ETL/ELT, governance and metadata controls.

## Interview language that scores higher
Use phrases like:
- "I owned the architecture decision and delivery guardrails."
- "I implemented controls for quality, lineage, and operational monitoring."
- "I translated platform work into business outcomes and risk reduction."
- "I can operate as an individual contributor while building a future team model."

## What to avoid
- Overly generic architecture talk with no measurable outcomes.
- Listing tools without explaining design decisions.
- Saying "we" for everything; clarify your personal contribution.
- Long technical detail without linking to business value.

## Your opening pitch (45 seconds)
"I am a hands-on data and integration leader with 15+ years across engineering, BI, and architecture in Australia and New Zealand. I have led enterprise modernization programs including architecture across 250+ endpoints, Fabric medallion implementations, Power BI enterprise uplift, and large-scale migration such as 8 TB SQL Server to Azure. What fits this role is that I can operate as the first data leader: assess current state, define a pragmatic roadmap, and personally deliver the foundation for MDM, integration, governance, and trusted analytics while partnering directly with the CIO and business stakeholders."
