# Senior Data Architect - Job Description

## Project Overview
The Bluegum Digital Health Transformation Program is responsible for delivering the Digital Health Transformation Strategy, a 10-year program of work that aims to transform the state's health system into a world-class, innovative and integrated system designed to empower consumers and enable healthcare professionals to deliver better patient outcomes through system-wide, digitally enabled technologies.

The Bluegum Digital Health Transformation Program, including the implementation of the Electronic Medical Record (EMR) system, is essential for efficient delivery of frontline health services into the future. The program aims to significantly enhance healthcare for all people in Tasmania by creating a more connected and accessible health system, ensuring that all Tasmanians receive the care they need, in the right place, at the right time, ultimately improving health outcomes across the state.

The Senior Data Architect will lead the design, creation, management of the way data will be structured, stored, used and managed in IT systems.

This role will implement the data architecture for enablement of Tasmania's Health Information Exchange (HIE), and the financial and operational reporting across the Department. This role ensures that health data structure is standardised, interoperable, secure, and accessible across the state's healthcare systems.

## Experience / Skills

### Essential Experience and Skills
1. Tertiary Qualifications: Undergraduate qualification in computers or IT such as Information Technology, Computer Science, Data Science, Information Systems or equivalent.
2. Data Architecture Experience: Minimum of eight years' experience designing and implementing scalable enterprise data architectures for large and complex organisations.
3. Health Sector Experience: Minimum of five years' experience within the Australian healthcare sector.
4. Healthcare Data Modelling: Experience data modelling clinical healthcare data with a minimum of either clinical data or healthcare billing data.
5. Data Modelling: Strong skills in conceptual, logical, and physical data modelling.
6. Data Lakes: Experience with large-scale data warehouse design and optimisation including Microsoft Fabric OneLake.
7. Data Patterns: Knowledge and significant experience defining data migration and engineering patterns over both on-premises source systems and cloud-hosted platforms; enterprise data pattern of medallion architecture; snowflake and star schemas.
8. Microsoft Skills: Advanced proficiency with Microsoft Azure and the Microsoft Fabric platform, including proven experience developing Enterprise Fabric architecture blueprints.
9. Data Governance: Experience creating and implementing data governance frameworks (especially considering clinical data lifecycle), standards, data dictionaries, and policies with monitoring practices to ensure data accuracy, security and quality.
10. Metadata Management: Knowledge of cataloguing, lineage, and data stewardship practices, optimise performance, cost management, and scalability.
11. Healthcare Data Standards: Knowledge of healthcare data standards including HL7 v2/v3, FHIR, CDA, SNOWMED-CT and other healthcare interoperability standards.
12. Security and Privacy: Knowledge of healthcare data privacy regulations (e.g., HIPAA, GDPR, Australian Privacy Principles) and experience implementing secure data exchange protocols (e.g., OAuth2, SAML, TLS).

## Purpose and Deliverables
- The Senior Data Architect reports to the Director of Architecture.
- Contracted Services are managed on a kanban card within the Bluegum Architecture Services team kanban board.
- Contracted Services include, but are not limited to:
  1. Author and update ICT Architecture artefacts of the Bluegum architecture team including technical architecture, system design and specifications, contribution to procurement activities, option papers, and integration plans.
  2. Contributing to continuous improvement of the Bluegum Architecture Services team's ways of working.
  3. Continuous improvement to architecture templates in the knowledge base.
  4. Adherence to architecture design principles, quality standards and best practices of Bluegum and HICT.
  5. Identify potential risks associated with the implementation of IT solutions and work to mitigate these risks.
  6. Contribute to, participate in and lead discussions within architecture governance forums within Bluegum and HICT.
- Escalate any risks, issues or concerns directly to the Director of Architecture.
- As a member of the digital architect team, collaborate and pair work to accelerate delivery and share knowledge.
- The specified personnel may be given duties beyond the contract services defined, as deemed necessary by the Director of Architecture.
